Natural Language Processing (NLP) is a branch of artificial intelligence that helps computers understand, interpret, and generate human language.

Tokenization is the process of breaking text into individual words or sentences called tokens. For example "I love NLP" becomes ["I", "love", "NLP"].

Stop Words are common words like "the", "is", "at" that are removed during text processing because they carry little meaning.

Stemming reduces words to their root form by removing suffixes. For example "running" becomes "run" and "cats" becomes "cat".

Lemmatization is similar to stemming but uses vocabulary and grammar rules to get the correct base form. "better" becomes "good" with lemmatization.

Bag of Words is a text representation technique that counts word frequency in a document ignoring grammar and word order.

TF-IDF stands for Term Frequency Inverse Document Frequency. It measures how important a word is in a document relative to a collection of documents.

Word2Vec is a technique that represents words as dense vectors where similar words have similar vectors. It captures semantic relationships between words.

GloVe stands for Global Vectors for Word Representation. It combines global word co-occurrence statistics with local context to create word embeddings.

BERT stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers. It reads text in both directions to understand context better than previous models.

Transformers are deep learning models that use self-attention mechanisms to process sequential data. They are the foundation of modern NLP models like GPT and BERT.

Sentiment Analysis is the task of identifying whether text expresses positive, negative, or neutral sentiment. Used in social media monitoring and product reviews.

Text Classification assigns predefined categories to text documents. Examples include spam detection, topic classification, and language detection.

Named Entity Recognition (NER) identifies and classifies named entities in text such as person names, organizations, locations, and dates.

Machine Translation automatically converts text from one language to another. Examples include Google Translate and DeepL.
